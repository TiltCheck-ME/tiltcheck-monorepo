## 1. Who We Are

TiltCheck is not a corporate tool.

It is a **community-born degen intelligence system** built by someone who actually understands the problems casinos, bots, bonus cycles, and users create. Built by a solo founder — a problem-solver, builder, and degen — who has lived the same experiences: bonus cycles, rage bets, tilt, payout delays, shady promos, predatory loyalty programs, and math mismatches.

**The persona:** A millennial degen who has been around the block. Chased a loss streak at 3am. Held a bag too long. Also had that one night where they cashed out at exactly the right moment and it felt incredible. They are not naive about gambling. They just genuinely do not want to see their friends get wrecked by the math.

**One-line summary:** Your smartest friend who also went to stats class and actually paid attention.

**Tagline (footer use only, not headlines):** Made for Degens. By Degens.

## 2. Brand Pillars

### Degen Empathy
TiltCheck does not preach. It does not moralize. It does not try to stop people from gambling. It is honest because it has lived the same experiences.

### Transparency Over Hype
Casinos love opacity. TiltCheck hates it. The brand values fairness, clarity, evidence, and user empowerment. When a casino is shady, TiltCheck calls it out. When users behave recklessly, TiltCheck nudges — with humor, not shame.

### Fun Is Mandatory
TiltCheck is serious about infrastructure but playful in tone. Humor is part of the brand DNA. Sarcastic asides, clever one-liners, degen memes. But never cruel. Never judgmental. Always community-first.

### Non-Custodial Forever
TiltCheck will never hold or control user funds.

> "Your crypto stays yours. We do not babysit it."

The brand loudly supports: user-owned wallets, transparent swaps, no-custody tipping, crystal-clear fee structure.

### Built by a Solo Founder
TiltCheck is founder-built. Not a committee. Not a VC-backed, over-engineered corporate project. Just a builder, a problem-solver, a degen, and someone who loves making tools work better. The tone reflects this authenticity.

## 3. Voice & Tone

### The Persona in One Paragraph

Smart-mouthed but not mean. Blunt, not clinical. Caring under the snark — the whole point is to help people keep their wins, the soft heart is always there, it just does not lead with sentimentality. Self-aware. The brand knows gambling is complicated and does not pretend otherwise.

### Tone Scale by Context

| Context | Tone Level | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Critical alert / loss streak | Blunt + urgent | "You have lost 3 in a row. The math is not going to flip for you right now. Walk away." |
| Standard nudge | Casual + direct | "Hey. Your session has been going for 2 hours. Just check in with yourself." |
| Tough love nudge | Smart-ass + caring | "Still going? Bold. The house appreciates your commitment." |
| Success / win secured | Warm + celebratory | "That is what we are here for. You actually kept it. Legendary." |
| Error / downtime | Dry humor + honest | "The API is being annoying. Showing you cached scores from earlier. Real data back soon." |
| Empty states | Wry + encouraging | "Nothing here yet. Give it time — the bad decisions will show up eventually." |
| Legal / compliance | Plain + grounded | "We do not touch your money. We do not hold your keys. We just send signals. That is it." |
| 404 / broken pages | Self-deprecating wit | "Jme pushed broken code to prod again. It happens. Let us get you back." |

**The humor gradient rule:** Humor escalates *down* as the stakes escalate *up*.

## 4. Copy Rules (The Laws)

### DO

- Write like a person, not a product. Contractions are fine. Short sentences are fine.
- Name the feeling. "We know chasing losses at midnight feels logical. It is not."
- Credit the user's intelligence. They know what they are doing. We are just here with better data.
- Use "you" freely. Talk directly to the person.
- Use humor as a tool, not decoration. A well-timed joke can break a spiral better than a warning ever will.
- Get serious when it is actually serious. A 5-loss streak with a large bet is not the moment for a punchline.
- Match the smart-ass energy to the moment. Early in a session = chatty. Mid-spiral = pointed. Full tilt = no jokes, just the truth.

### DO NOT

- Use cold system-speak for user-facing copy. `AUDIT_COMPLETE: PASS` is for internal logs, not users.
- Apologize. Replace "We are sorry, the API is down" with "The data is taking a breather. Showing you the last known scores."
- Lecture. One point, one message. Not preachy. Say it once.
- Punch at the user. Roast the situation, the casino, the math. Never the person themselves.
- Use emojis in the web UI. Discord bot can use them sparingly. Web UI is clean.
- Use profanity casually or decoratively. Strategic use only — where it lands with intention.
- Be funny when someone is actually in crisis. If they have lost real money and are in distress, drop the wit and be a person.

### Banned Phrases (Auto-Reject)

- "Sorry, but..." / "We apologize..." / "Please..." (as a softener)
- "We hope you enjoy using TiltCheck responsibly."
- "Unfortunately, we could not..."
- "Please be mindful of your spending habits."
- Any copy that could belong to any generic app

## 5. Wit & Humor Framework

Humor is a de-escalation tool. When someone in a losing streak smirks at a line, their tunnel vision breaks for a second. That second is when they might actually stop and think. That is the whole point.

### Three Types of Humor

**1. Self-deprecating (about us / the system)**
We are allowed to be fallible. Jme breaks prod. The API goes down. We say so.
> "The API is having a moment. Same tbh. Here is what we had from earlier."
> "Jme pushed to prod on a Friday again. 404 life. Let us get you home."

**2. Situational / Observational (about the behavior, not the person)**
Clock what is happening and reflect it back with a raised eyebrow, not a finger-wag.
> "Bold of you to hit that bet right now. Anyway. Your loss count is at 4."
> "The house has officially entered its winning streak era. You might want to sit this one out."

**3. Warm roast (tough love tier)**
This has bite, but the bite comes from actually giving a damn.
> "You have been in the arena for 3 hours. Your brain is cooked. Go eat something."
> "Oh you are revenge betting. How is that going for you statistically? Yeah. Thought so."
> "The last 5 bets say you are not okay. Not judging — just pointing at the data."

### When NOT to Be Funny

- Active crisis / critical tilt at high stakes
- Legal and compliance pages
- When the user has explicitly expressed distress
- Scam blocking warnings (dry is fine, jokey is not — someone almost lost their wallet)

### The Test

Read the line out loud. Would the person smirk and then actually consider stopping? Pass. Would it land as mean, dismissive, or like you are mocking them? Fail.

| "Cooldown period" | "Breather" / "Mandatory chill" |
| "Trust Score" | "your number" / "the score" |

### Degen Vocabulary (Use in copy and UI)

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `degen` | Noun ("you are a degen") AND verb ("to degen" = to gamble recklessly). Both forms valid. |
| `im due` | Gambler's fallacy. Correct gently. |

| `cooked` | In bad shape, mentally or financially. |
| `locked in` | Focused, committed, protecting the bag. |

### Signature Phrases (Use sparingly — only where they land)

- "The math is mathing." — when the data is clear and ugly.
- "Go touch grass." — affectionate push to step away.
- "Bag secured." — when a win is locked in. Celebratory.
- "The house loves this momentum." — when a negative pattern is detected.
- "We can count too." — founding thesis in four words.
- "Still have your wits about you?" — gentle check-in nudge.
- "Winning is easy. Keeping it is legendary." — for vault/redeem moments.
- "Bold of you." — dry acknowledgment of a risky move. Not mean, just honest.
- "How is that going for you statistically?" — smart-ass tough love. Use at moderate tilt.
- "The house has entered its winning streak era." — for sustained losing patterns.
- "You are not okay right now, and that is okay." — when the joke is done and it is time to be real.

### Under-Construction Phrases (WIP Features)

- **"GRID EXPANSION IN PROGRESS"** — for expanding features.

## 7. Surface-by-Surface Guide

### Web App (Hero + Pages)
- Headlines can be loud and expressive. (`THE HOUSE ALWAYS WINS? FUCK THAT.`)
- Body copy: conversational, short, no walls of text.
- Empty states: warmly encouraging, not clinical.
- Error states: dry humor + useful info. Never just a red box with jargon.

### Discord Bot Nudges
- Keep it tight. One message, one point.
- Match severity to tone: gentle = chatty, critical = punchy and urgent.
- Never robotic. Even at CRITICAL severity, it should feel like a friend who is worried.
- Emojis allowed here, but to land emotional tone — not as decoration.

### Chrome Extension
- Pop-up space is tiny. Be brutal with words.
- One-line alerts: direct, human, end with what action to take.
- Example: `You have been on Stake for 2 hours. Play one more, then step back.`

### Empty States & Placeholders
- No generic "No data found" messages.
- Add personality: `Nothing here yet — your activity log will fill up once you start playing.`

### Success States
- Celebrate with the user. This is a win.
- Example (vault secured): `Yep. That is what we are here for. You actually kept the money. Let us go.`

### Legal / Compliance Copy
- Plain, clear, calm. No clever tone here.
- Respect the user's time: get to the point, avoid legalese.
- Always follow with what this means in practice.
- Example: `We are non-custodial. That means we cannot touch your funds. Not would not — cannot. Your keys, your crypto.`

## 8. Visual Identity

### Design Philosophy

- Dark-first. Obsidian black backgrounds (#0a0c10).
- Clean lines. Zero-radius on brand UI elements (cards, inputs).
- Dashboard vibes — not glossy, not corporate, not over-designed.
- Everything should feel trustworthy, simple, and zero-to-tilt in 0.02 seconds.
- Minimalist. Let the content breathe.

### Canonical Style Reference

The `/microgrant` page (`tiltcheck.me/microgrant`) is the **canonical visual reference** for all non-hero pages. When in doubt, match that page's scale, typography, and spacing.

### Homepage Hero

The homepage hero (glitter background + neon title) is locked. Do not modify it. It is the flagship brand moment.

### Frosted Glass Treatment

Info boxes that sit over the glitter background use frosted glass:
- Background: `rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.42)`
- Blur: `backdrop-filter: blur(14px) saturate(1.2)`
- Edges fade into the background via `mask-image: radial-gradient(ellipse ...)`
- No hard border — soft glow shadow only

### Neon Glow

Teal neon glow is a brand accent. Use on primary headings, active states, and key CTAs. Classes: `.neon`, `.neon-main`, `.neon-sub`.

## 9. Typography

### Primary Font: Inter

- **Usage:** All body copy, UI labels, navigation, headings
- **Loaded via:** `next/font/google` (Inter, weights 300–900)
- **CSS variable:** `var(--font-geist-sans)` → `var(--font-inter), "Inter", "Segoe UI", Roboto, sans-serif`

### Monospace Font: JetBrains Mono

- **Usage:** Code blocks, audit labels, terminal-style UI elements, data readouts
- **Loaded via:** `next/font/google` (JetBrains Mono, weights 400–800)
- **CSS variable:** `var(--font-geist-mono)` → `var(--font-jetbrains-mono), "JetBrains Mono", Menlo, Monaco, monospace`

### Type Scale

| Role | Class | Style |
|---|---|---|
| Page eyebrow | `.brand-eyebrow` | JetBrains Mono, 0.7rem, uppercase, teal, letter-spacing 0.15em |
| Page title | `.brand-page-title` | Inter, clamp(2rem → 3.5rem), weight 900, white |
| Section heading | `.brand-section-title` | Inter, clamp(1.4rem → 2rem), weight 700, white |
| Body / intro | `.brand-body` | Inter, clamp(0.9rem → 1.1rem), #c4ced8 |
| Mono label | `.brand-mono-label` | JetBrains Mono, 0.75rem, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.12em |

### Rules

- Body font weight max: 400 (regular) for paragraphs, 900 for headings/CTAs
- No italic except hero audit banner body
- No underlines in navigation (use color + glow instead)

## 11. Governance & Enforcement

### The Degen Laws (Non-Negotiable)

Every PR and file change is subject to these laws. Violations block merges.

| Law | Rule | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Tone | Direct, witty, supportive tough love. No apologies, no fluff. | Medium → Critical |
| 2. No Emojis in Code | No emojis in source, comments, commits, or UI text. | Medium |
| 3. Copyright Headers | Every file must include `© 2024–2026 TiltCheck Ecosystem. All Rights Reserved. Last Updated: YYYY-MM-DD` | Medium |
| 4. Footer | Every user-facing UI must show "Made for Degens. By Degens." | High |
| 5. Atomic Docs | New features/APIs must include docs in the same PR. | High |
| 6. Non-Custodial | Never store private keys or hold user funds. Ever. | Critical |
| 7. SESSION_LOG.md | Every code PR must update SESSION_LOG.md with a dated, specific entry. | High |
| 8. No Secrets | No credentials, API keys, or sensitive data in commits. | Critical |
| 9. Test Coverage | New business logic requires >70% test coverage. | High |

### Header Format

**TypeScript / JavaScript / TSX:**

// © 2024–2026 TiltCheck Ecosystem. All Rights Reserved. Last Updated: YYYY-MM-DD
