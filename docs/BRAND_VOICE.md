# TiltCheck Brand Voice & Copy Guidelines

**Version 1.1 — The Degen Standard (Wit Edition)**

> This is the single source of truth for how TiltCheck speaks across every surface: web, Discord, extension pop-ups, error pages, nudges, and docs. Inconsistency is a trust killer. Read this before you write a single word of copy.

---

## 1. The Persona

**Who is TiltCheck?**

Think of a **millennial degen who's been around the block**. They've chased a loss streak at 3am, they've held a bag way too long, they've also had that one night where they cashed out at the right moment and it *felt incredible*. They're not naive about gambling — they get it. They just genuinely don't want to see their friends get wrecked by the math.

TiltCheck's voice is:

- **Smart-mouthed but not mean.** It roasts the situation, not the person.
- **Blunt, not clinical.** We don't use corporate jargon. We use real talk.
- **Caring under the snark.** The whole point is to help people keep their wins. The soft heart is always there — it just doesn't lead with sentimentality.
- **Self-aware.** We know gambling is complicated. We don't pretend otherwise.

**The one-line summary:** We are your smartest friend who also went to stats class and actually paid attention.

---

## 2. Tone of Voice Scale

Use this spectrum depending on context:

| Context | Tone Level | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Critical alert / loss streak | **Blunt + urgent** | "You've lost 3 in a row. The math is not going to flip for you right now. Walk away." |
| Standard nudge | **Casual + direct** | "Hey. Your session's been going for 2 hours. Just... check in with yourself." |
| **Tough love nudge** | **Smart-ass + caring** | "Still going? Bold. The house appreciates your commitment." |
| Success / win secured | **Warm + celebratory** | "That's what we're here for. You actually kept it. Legendary." |
| Error / downtime | **Dry humor + honest** | "The API is being annoying. We're showing you cached scores from earlier. Real data back soon." |
| Empty states | **Wry + encouraging** | "Nothing here yet. Give it time — the bad decisions will show up eventually." |
| Legal / compliance | **Plain + grounded** | "We don't touch your money. We don't hold your keys. We just send signals. That's it." |
| 404 / broken pages | **Self-deprecating wit** | "Jme pushed broken code to prod again. It happens. Let's get you back." |

## 3. Core Rules (The Laws)

### ✅ DO

- **Write like a person, not a product.** Contractions are fine. Short sentences are fine.
- **Name the feeling.** "We know chasing losses at midnight feels logical. It isn't."
- **Credit the user's intelligence.** They know what they're doing. We're just here with better data.
- **Use "you" freely.** Talk directly to the person.
- **Use humor as a tool, not decoration.** A well-timed joke can break a spiral better than a warning ever will. If they laugh, they paused. That's the point.
- **Get serious when it's actually serious.** A 5-loss streak with a large bet is not the moment for a punchline.
- **Match the smart-ass energy to the moment.** Early in a session = chatty. Mid-spiral = pointed. Full tilt = no jokes, just the truth.

### ❌ DON'T

- **Don't use cold system-speak for user-facing copy.** `AUDIT_COMPLETE: PASS` is for internal logs, not users.
- **Don't apologize.** Replace "We're sorry, the API is down" with "The data's taking a breather. Showing you the last known scores."
- **Don't lecture.** One point, one message. We're not preachy. We say it once.
- **Don't punch at the user.** Roast the situation, the casino, the math. Never the person themselves.
- **Don't use emojis in the web UI.** Discord bot can use them sparingly. Web UI is clean.
- **Don't use profanity casually or decoratively.** "FUCK THAT" on the hero headline is intentional and powerful. It doesn't work everywhere.
- **Don't be funny when someone's actually in crisis.** If they've lost real money and are in distress, drop the wit and just be a person.

## 4. Wit & Humor — How It Works

Humor is a **de-escalation tool**. When someone's in a losing streak and we say something that makes them smirk, we've broken their tunnel vision for a second. That second is when they might actually stop and think. This is the whole point.

### The Three Types of Humor We Use

**1. Self-deprecating (about us / the system)**
We're allowed to be fallible. Jme breaks prod. The API goes down. We say so.
> `"The API is having a moment. Same tbh. Here's what we had from earlier."`
> `"Jme pushed to prod on a Friday again. 404 life. Let's get you home."`

**2. Situational / Observational (about the behavior, not the person)**
We clock what's happening and reflect it back with a raised eyebrow, not a finger-wag.
> `"Bold of you to hit that bet right now. Anyway. Your loss count is at 4."`
> `"The house has officially entered its 'winning streak' era. You might want to sit this one out."`
> `"Still going? Respect the commitment. The math doesn't, but we do."`

**3. Warm roast (the 'I say this because I care' energy)**
This is the tough love tier. It has bite, but the bite comes from a place of actually giving a shit.
> `"You've been in the arena for 3 hours. Your brain is cooked. Go eat something."`
> `"Oh you're revenge betting. How's that going for you statistically? Yeah. Thought so."`
> `"The last 5 bets say you're not okay. Not judging — just pointing at the data."`

### When NOT to be Funny
- Active crisis / critical tilt at high stakes
- Legal and compliance pages
- When the user has explicitly expressed distress
- Scam blocking (the warning page can be dry, but not jokey — someone almost lost their wallet)

### The Test
Read the line out loud. Would the person smirk and then actually consider stopping? ✅
Would it land as mean, dismissive, or like you're mocking them? ❌

## 5. Key Phrases & Vocabulary

### Preferred Terms

| Instead of... | Use... |
|---|---|
| "Error" | "Something's off" / "That didn't work" |
| "User" | "you" / "degen" (in casual contexts) |
| "Authentication failed" | "We don't recognize that wallet. Try again." |
| "Session terminated" | "We locked the session. For your sake." |
| "Access denied" | "This part's invite-only." |
| "Algorithm" | "the math" |
| "Threshold exceeded" | "You've crossed your own line" |
| "Cooldown period" | "Breather" / "Mandatory chill" |
| "Trust Score" | "your number" / "the score" |

### Signature Phrases (Use sparingly — only where they land)

- **"The math is mathing."** — when the data is clear and ugly.
- **"Go touch grass."** — a genuine, affectionate push to step away.
- **"Bag secured."** — when a win is locked in. Celebratory.
- **"The house loves this momentum."** — when a negative pattern is detected.
- **"We can count too."** — our founding thesis in four words.
- **"Still have your wits about you?"** — gentle check-in nudge.
- **"Winning is easy. Keeping it is legendary."** — for vault/redeem moments.
- **"Bold of you."** — dry acknowledgment of a risky move. Not mean, just honest.
- **"How's that going for you statistically?"** — the smart-ass tough love line. Use at moderate tilt.
- **"The house has entered its winning streak era."** — for sustained losing patterns. Funny, not cruel.
- **"You're not okay right now, and that's okay."** — for the moment when the joke is done and it's time to be real.

## 6. Surface-by-Surface Guidelines

### 6a. Web App (Hero + Pages)

- Headlines can be loud and expressive (`THE HOUSE ALWAYS WINS? FUCK THAT.`).
- Body copy should be conversational and short. No walls of text.
- Empty states should be warmly encouraging, not clinical.
- Error states: dry humor + useful info. Never just a red box with jargon.

### 5b. Discord Bot Nudges

- Keep it tight. One message, one point.
- Match severity to tone: gentle = chatty, critical = punchy and urgent.
- **Never** feel robotic. Even at CRITICAL severity, it should feel like a friend who's worried, not a banking app.
- Emojis are allowed here, but used to land emotional tone — not as decoration.

### 5c. Chrome Extension

- Pop-up space is tiny. Be brutal with words.
- One-line alerts: direct, human, end with what action to take.
- Example: `You've been on Stake for 2 hours. Play one more, then step back.`

### 5d. Empty States & Placeholders

- Don't leave generic "No data found" messages.
- Add a tiny bit of personality: `Nothing here yet — your activity log will fill up once you start playing.`

### 5e. Success States

- Celebrate with the user. This is a win.
- Example (vault secured): `"Yep. That's what we're here for. You actually kept the money. Let's go."`

### 5f. Legal / Compliance Copy

- Be plain, clear, and calm. No clever tone here.
- Respect the user's time: get to the point, avoid legalese.
- Always follow with what this means in practice.
- Example: `We're non-custodial. That means we cannot touch your funds. Not won't — can't. Your keys, your crypto.`

## 6. Colors as Communication

The visual language reinforces the voice. Use colors intentionally:

| Color | Meaning | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Teal `#17c3b2`** | Neutral / Active / Default | Standard UI, live indicators, primary CTA |
| **Gold `#ffd700`** | Achievement / Premium / High Trust | Elite scores (85+), vault secured, milestone moments |
| **Red `#ef4444`** | Danger / Urgent | Loss alerts, critical tilt signals, genuine warnings |
| **White / Gray** | Information / Secondary | Body copy, secondary labels |

**Do NOT use purple `#8b5cf6` or magenta `#d946ef`** outside of legacy components actively being phased out.

## 7. Character Reference: "The TiltCheck Moment"

Any piece of copy that genuinely embodies this voice should feel like someone sent you this in iMessage at 12:45am:

> "bro. you've been at it for 3 hours. your session stats are cooked. the math is not going to flip. take the 15 bucks you have left and call it a night. seriously. we're proud of you for even looking at this tool."

That vulnerability + honesty + stats-backed directness is the TiltCheck moment. Aim for it.

### The Humor Gradient — A Practical Example

Same scenario (2-hour session, 3 losses), different moments:

| Moment | Copy |
|---|---|
| First check-in (gentle) | `"Two hours in. How you feeling? The math says... moderate chaos."` |
| Second check-in (smart-ass) | `"Still here? The house noticed. They sent a thank-you card."` |
| Third check-in (firm) | `"Okay. Your session has been running 3 hours and you're down bad. Not the time for jokes. What's your exit plan?"` |
| Genuine crisis (no humor) | `"Hey. Stop for a second. You've crossed your own line. Talk to your Guardian or go to tiltcheck.me/touch-grass."` |

The humor escalates *down* as the stakes escalate *up*. That's the rule.
